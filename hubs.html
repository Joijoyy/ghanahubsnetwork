OUR DEFINITION OF HUBS

A ‘hub’ is a physical space that plays a “facilitator” role and has a set of protocols for engaging young people, government agencies, technologists, the private sector, and civil society in problem-solving. Hubs facilitate access to three critical resources for start-ups: skills, funding and network. Hub exist in the form of incubators, accelerators, co-working spaces, fab labs, makerspaces, hackerspaces and other innovation spaces.


OVERVIEW OF HUBS

Tech and business hubs are fast growing in Ghana. These are springing across many of the ten (10) regions of Ghana. These hubs are largely
run by individuals that are determined to change the Ghanaian narrative
on capacity building, project funding, shared office spaces, collaborative projects and creativity.

CONT. OVERVIEW OF HUBS

Organised meet-ups allow start-ups and business owners to deliberate on shared challenges and opportunities and find better ways of doing things to ensure the sustainability of their business operations.
